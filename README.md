# 📊 Employee Attrition Analysis

## 🔍 Project Objective
The objective of this project is to analyze employee attrition data and identify the key factors contributing to employee turnover within an organization.

This analysis helps HR and management teams understand patterns behind attrition and make data-driven retention decisions.

---

## 🛠 Tools & Technologies Used
- Python
- Pandas
- Matplotlib
- Seaborn
- Jupyter Notebook

---

## 📁 Dataset Description
The dataset contains employee-level information including:
- Age
- Department
- Job Role
- Monthly Income
- Work Life Balance
- Years at Company
- Overtime Status
- Attrition (Yes/No)

---

## 📈 Key Analysis Performed

### 1️⃣ Overall Attrition Rate
- Calculated distribution of employees who stayed vs left.

### 2️⃣ Overtime Impact
- Compared attrition rate for employees working overtime.

### 3️⃣ Salary Analysis
- Analyzed Monthly Income distribution across attrition groups.

### 4️⃣ Department-Level Analysis
- Identified departments with higher turnover.

### 5️⃣ Work-Life Balance Analysis
- Examined how work-life balance relates to attrition.

### 6️⃣ Experience & Tenure Analysis
- Studied relationship between Years at Company and attrition.

---

## 💡 Key Insights

- Employees working overtime show significantly higher attrition.
- Lower-income employees are more likely to leave.
- Employees with fewer years at the company have higher turnover rates.
- Certain departments show higher attrition compared to others.
- Work-life balance plays a measurable role in retention.

---

## 🏢 Business Recommendations

- Re-evaluate overtime policies.
- Improve compensation strategies for high-risk groups.
- Implement early-stage employee engagement programs.
- Develop department-specific retention strategies.

---

## 🚀 Conclusion
This project demonstrates how exploratory data analysis can uncover actionable business insights and support strategic HR decision-making.
